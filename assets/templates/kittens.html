Kittens

Catcam has been disabled, the kittens are no longer confined to their old temporary play area and have been able to roam around freely indoors for a few weeks. They spend very little time in view of the camera now, and even when the old play area gate is put back in place they can just jump over it. So instead of the live camera, pictures I have taken and videos from multiple sources will be added to this page so the kittens can still be seen at any time.

More Info

Who are these kittens?

A wild cat in the area chose to have her kittens here outside my house.

The kittens are being brought indoors and socialized around people in order to keep them from becoming wild cats like their mother, and so they can have better lives than they would if they remained outdoors.

When were they born?

They were born May 30th or 31st, 2013.

Are the kittens or their mother being fed?

The mother and the kittens are all being fed dry food and provided with clean water.

Are the kittens going to have homes?

Three kittens will stay here (see chart above: Fozzie, Nermal and tiger #3), the others will be going to new homes when they're ready.

Kitten Blog

September 13th, 2013

The live cat cam video stream has been turned off since they are never on camera anymore, they roam the house and sleep all over the place whenever they want. They can't be confined to their old play area anymore even when the barrier is put up, they just jump over it.

The tan kitten has been named Fozzie, the grey kitten has been named Nermal (from Garfield), and the others don't have names at the moment aside from the identifying names I have for them (black kitty, sock kitty, wild kitty etc).

With the live camera turned off I'll start filling this page with all the pictures and videos I have so far that were never posted anywhere, aside from a select few that made their way to Youtube.

August 3rd, 2013

Tan kitty and some co-conspirators figured out how to get up on the cabinet visible in the upper right corner of the video, which made it easy for them to walk right across to the kitchen counter. It won't be long before they can all just jump over the barriers keeping them in their current area, which means they may not be in front of the camera much longer.

August 1st, 2013

Every kitten is tame now, the last 2 to be brought inside are still a little suspicious of people but none of them hiss, bite, growl or act aggressively anymore for any reason.

The grey kitten, which is very close to being named 'grey kitty' by default, is the lap cat now. Grey kitty walks around purring when people are around, and immediately purrs when picked up and held.

They have all discovered climbing things, and have figured out how to climb up on the wooden ledge under the windows so they can look out in the yard and driveway. The windows are never opened so they're safe sitting up there.

July 20th, 2013

All 6 kittens are in the big play area now, which has increased the crazy level by 1,000%. The 2 new ones started playing and running around almost immediately.

The original 4 are well adjusted to being around people now, the solid grey kitten is still a little suspicious but doesn't hiss or run away anymore, and the other 3 are climbing on people and falling asleep in laps, they already love being petted.

July 16th, 2013

The 4 kittens that are indoors already have been moved from their small temporary cage to their new big area. They have lots of space to play and run as fast as they can chasing each other, toys to play with, boxes to climb around on, and lots of exploring to do.

They should be visible on the camera most of the time now, there are some blind spots but when they are playing they play out in the middle where you can see them.

July 14th, 2013

A fourth kitten, the solid grey with blue eyes, has been brought inside today. It was not happy about it at all, but it should adjust over the next few days just like the others.

The first two kittens are both completely adjusted, playing and eating on their own. The tiger wants to be petted and played with all the time, and comes to people without hesitation.

July 13th, 2013

A third kitten was brought inside today and put with the other two from yesterday. The new arrival is quite angry but will quickly forget and adjust to being inside.

The two that came inside yesterday haven't hissed at all today, they have adjusted quite well already. They're playing, eating food, and one is licking my finger.

July 12th, 2013

Two kittens were brought inside today. It's a challenge finding chances while their mom is gone, catching them before they can find a hiding spot in a bush without being bitten by their little baby teeth, getting them into a safe box and bringing them inside. Four remain outside, but not for long.

They will be kept in a small area where they will probably huddle together for a few days until they adjust to being inside, but they're able to eat even solid food now so they should do just fine.

July 11th, 2013

The tan kitten figured out how to climb over the fence this morning, then frantically tried to get back inside the fenced area with the rest of the kittens, the food and of course, mom. After about 5 minutes, the kitten climbed right back up the fence and back inside.

July 9th, 2013

Three kittens escaped their fenced in area today with encouragement from their mom, video shows her hopping up on top of the front porch barrier, then turning around and encouraging the kittens to climb up and escape. Only 3 tried to climb out, but because the barrier had small spaces a kitten could fit through, all 3 who tried got away for a while.

The 3 who escaped followed their mom down the front steps into the driveway, where she then put them aside under some bushes to sleep while she sat under a vehicle 5 feet away. She gave away their location by sitting there, and once I realized they were gone I was able to find them in just a few minutes.

All 3 escapees were brought back to their fenced in area and the spaces they escaped through were completely blocked. Large sheets of smooth plastic prevent them from being able to climb up on the porch again.

July 3rd, 2013

The kittens new area has been fenced in, which you can see on the right side of the live stream. Chicken wire fencing goes around 2 sides, and the 3rd side is the raised front porch, which has been blocked with various large objects to keep them from climbing up there and getting out.

The kittens should remain in this area until they are brought inside. It's safe, mostly dry and they can run and play without getting hurt in the driveway or street.

July 1st, 2013

Their Mom moved them yet again, but this time I watched her do it so I know where they went, 10 feet down the side of the house under another bush. It's a perfect place to fence them in and keep track of them so they can't be moved again until they're ready to come inside.

It's also suitable for a camera so that has been moved now too, it's pointing down at their play area. They won't be visible all the time but when they come out to eat they usually stay out for a while.

June 24th, 2013

The kittens mom moved them again to the front side of the house, under a large tree and a lot of bushes, it's dry and safe but too close to the driveway, they'll have to be moved somewhere else when they start walking around.

June 22nd, 2013

The kittens were moved by their mother today, out of the window well and over to a bush against the house. Not as protected from rain and animals but accessible so we can get to them. As they are no longer in a place suitable for a camera, it has been turned off for now. When they end up somewhere more permanent the camera can probably be turned back on.

June 19th, 2013

All the kittens have their eyes open today, and have kept them open. They are looking around and looking at me when I go see them through the window.

May 31st, 2013

A mother cat and 6 obviously newborn kittens were found living in a window well here, on a soft and dry pile of leaves. I didn't want to disturb them very much so I installed a video camera to keep track of them.
